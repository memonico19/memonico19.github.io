Husker Baseball has already done the impossible. Two years ago they won the B1G Tournament for the first time and created an atmosphere never before seen around their program. Last year, against all odds, they beat Oregon, the B1G season champion, and UCLA, the future 2025 CWS representative, to take the B1G Tournament crown again. With a slightly above average season in 2025, players like Robby Bolin, Cael Frost and Gabe Swanson helped the team come from behind to take the victory.

This year, the team has already made more of a mark than the last two years combined. Senior shortstop Dylan Carey surpassed the All-Time Career Doubles record, previously set by head coach Will Bolt. New transfer Mac Moyer is second on the B1G leader board for batting average, with Carey securing the No. 5 spot. Sophomore pitcher Carson Jasa is also second on the B1G leader board for most strikeouts, while Gavin Blachowicz is ranked fifth in the ERA standings. The team has so much to be proud of, yet so much to accomplish with the tournament right around the corner. There is history still to be made with the possibility of being the first team to ever have a 3-peat in the B1G Tournament. The only big obstacle: UCLA.

If Nebraska’s season was historic, UCLA’s has been revolutionary. They have topped the NCAA baseball charts all year. With only two losses this year, none have been in-conference, making them the undisputed top dog. Why is that? What makes them so hard to topple? And can Nebraska be the one to do it to make their dreams come true? Let’s see what the hitting tells us.

Although UCLA is No. 1 in the polls, Nebraska is No. 1 in batting average. With an average of 0.315, they beat out every other team by a recognizable amount, while UCLA sits with a 0.303. That is great, good for Nebraska. Yet, if you look closely, UCLA has more RBIs. So the next thing you have to think about is what you want to make an argument for. Runs win a the game. That is literally how you win, no one can deny that, but their hitting is not as consistent. There is an case to be made that consistent hitting, that gets you on base, will eventually find equilibrium with your RBIs. All you need is the next hit, and there is officially no better team in the B1G who does that than Nebraska.

There is another side we have not looked at yet: the pitching. When there is an argument to made about the importance of hitting, there is an even bigger one to be made about the importance of pitching. You cannot go anywhere without good pitching. And Nebraska will not beat UCLA without it. Will the starters be able to get the job done?

Logan Reddmann. That is all there is to say. He is the top pitcher in the NCAA right now. His walk-to-strike ratio is unheard of and every other pitcher wishes he has the attraction to the strike zone that Reddmann has. His 18-strikeout game against Rutgers will go down in the Bruin history books. He takes the top spot, obviously, but after more observation, Nebraska holds the next four spots. Our four starters throughout the year put distance between UCLA’s No. 1 and their number two. Jasa is biting at the heels of Reddmann’s strikeout number, while Katskee has almost thrown the same amount of walks. Every starting Husker pitcher that would face the Bruin batters is right up there with Reddmann. Seems like a big testament to their consistency. The other two weekend starters for UCLA remain at the bottom of the graph. Interesting.

Comparing the top two teams in the B1G can tell us a lot, but we need to also know about the success in the past two years that got Husker Baseball to where they are today. The bats have been on fire this year with multiple grand slams and at least one home run every game by Jett Buck or Drew Grego. How did that impact effect the past two Nebraska teams in their standings? Let’s find out.

There is a lot to sift through here. In 2024, Nebraska was never in a better condition. They were fifth overall on the season, with just below 75 total home runs. 2025 was the complete opposite. They were actually sixth to last. Wait, but that graph looks different. Here is why: on August 4, 2024, UCLA, Oregon, USC, Washington, Indiana and a few other teams officially joined the B1G conference. That is why Nebraska was so high in 2024, and why they were so low in 2025. New powerhouses were introduced. Throughout the 41 games played in this current season, Nebraska has an okay showing, but UCLA and Oregon are sending balls into the parking lot game after game. That is not something that Nebraska can compete with at the moment.

So, what is to make of this? Nebraska has the consistent hitting and pitching to take the B1G Tournament title right out of UCLA’s hands, but do they have the power hitters? In my opinion, a consistent team always pulls through. We will find out next month.
